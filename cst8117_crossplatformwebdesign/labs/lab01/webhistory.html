History of the Internet

The internet plays a huge part of our lives. It is almost impossible to find someone who doesn't use the internet regularly. Young people use it to search for educational information through search engines and to stay in touch with their friends through social media. Employers and employees use emails to communicate with each other and online tools to organize and store data.

We've become even more dependent on the internet due to the lockdown caused by the global pandemic. Schools have switched to online learning; some employees were forced to work from home. We use the internet daily, but who invented the internet?

No single person can be credited for the invention of the internet. It was created by many scientists and engineers. Each of them innovated ideas and methods to contribute to the internet we know now.

The first internet prototype "used packet switching to allow multiple computers to communicate on a single network." It then developed into allowing communication within multiple networks. (Andrews, 2013)
